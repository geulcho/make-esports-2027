# WT, World Tournaments + VSC, Viktor Sandberg Cup Format PRD

**Document version:** v1.1  
**Primary tournament:** WT, World Tournaments  
**Secondary tournament:** VSC, Viktor Sandberg Cup  
**VSC name origin:** Named after game developer Viktor Sandberg  
**Calendar window:** Week 39 to Week 43  
**Selected WT finish date:** Week 43 Sunday  
**WT participants:** 32 teams  
**VSC final tournament participants:** 16 teams  
**Purpose:** WT is the end-of-year highest-prestige global championship. VSC is a parallel second-tier cup for strong teams that missed WT and for teams finishing 3rd in WT groups.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

WT and VSC run inside the same Week 39 to Week 43 global championship window.

WT remains the main event:

1. **WT Group Stage**
   - 32 teams
   - 8 groups of 4 teams
   - Bo1 double round robin
   - Top 2 from each group advance to WT Knockout
   - 3rd place from each group moves into VSC Final Tournament

2. **WT Knockout Stage**
   - 16-team single elimination bracket
   - Round of 16, Quarterfinals, Semifinals, Grand Final
   - All knockout matches are Bo5
   - Grand Final on Week 43 Sunday

VSC runs as a secondary competition:

1. **VSC Regional Qualifiers**
   - Held during Week 39 and Week 40 in regional venues
   - APAC: 8 teams, 2 qualify
   - EMEA: 16 teams, 4 qualify
   - AMERICA: 8 teams, 2 qualify
   - Total regional qualifier winners: 8 teams

2. **VSC Final Tournament**
   - Starts only after WT Group Stage is complete
   - 8 VSC qualifier winners + 8 WT group 3rd-place teams
   - 16-team single elimination bracket
   - Round of 16 is Bo3
   - Quarterfinals onward are Bo5
   - VSC Grand Final on Week 43 Saturday

Scheduling principle:

> WT has broadcast priority. VSC qualifiers may overlap with WT because they are regional events. The VSC final bracket is placed almost entirely on WT off-days or non-match days to avoid stealing spotlight from the WT main stage.

## 2. WT Qualification Allocation

WT has 32 total qualification slots.

| Source | Allocation | Total Slots |
|---|---:|---:|
| League coefficient ranks 1 to 4 | 3 teams per league | 12 |
| League coefficient ranks 5 to 8 | 2 teams per league | 8 |
| League coefficient ranks 9 to 16 | 1 team per league | 8 |
| Special champions | MM, APEX, EGT, COPA champions | 4 |
| **Total** | - | **32** |

### 2.1 League Coefficient Slots

| League Coefficient Rank | WT Slots |
|---:|---:|
| 1 to 4 | 3 slots |
| 5 to 8 | 2 slots |
| 9 to 16 | 1 slot |

### 2.2 Special Champion Slots

| Tournament | Slot Type |
|---|---|
| MM, Midseason Mayhem | Special champion slot |
| APEX, APAC regional championship | Special champion slot |
| EGT, EMEA regional championship | Special champion slot |
| COPA, AMERICA regional championship | Special champion slot |

### 2.3 Duplicate Qualification Handling

If a special champion has already qualified through its domestic league, or if a team wins multiple special-slot tournaments, the duplicate slot is transferred to the next-highest eligible team from the same league.

Recommended duplicate resolution order:

1. Lock normal league coefficient slots.
2. Apply MM champion slot.
3. Apply APEX champion slot.
4. Apply EGT champion slot.
5. Apply COPA champion slot.
6. For each duplicate, pass the slot to the next-highest eligible non-qualified team from the same league.

## 5. WT Group Stage Format

### 5.1 Basic Format

| Item | Value |
|---|---:|
| Groups | 8 |
| Teams per group | 4 |
| Format | Bo1 double round robin |
| Matches per group | 12 |
| Total group-stage matches | 96 Bo1 |
| WT advancement | Top 2 teams from each group, 16 total |
| VSC transfer | 3rd-place team from each group, 8 total |

### 5.2 Group Stage Scheduling Model

The group stage is split into two phases.

| Phase | Duration | Match Pattern |
|---|---:|---|
| First half | 6 days | Each day has 1 match from each group, for 8 Bo1 matches per day |
| Second half | 8 days | One group is completed per day, with 6 Bo1 matches for that group |

This creates a 14-day group stage:

- First 6 days: 48 Bo1 matches
- Next 8 days: 48 Bo1 matches
- Total: 96 Bo1 matches

### 5.3 Group Stage Tiebreakers

Group standings are determined by the following tiebreaker order:

| Priority | Criterion |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Match wins |
| 2 | Score differential |
| 3 | Score scored |
| 4 | Head-to-head result |
| 5 | Seed comparison |

No extra tiebreaker matches are scheduled by default.

| Code | VSC |
| Named after | Game developer Viktor Sandberg |
| Tournament tier | Second-tier global event during the WT window |
| Eligibility concept | Next-best teams that failed to qualify for WT, plus WT group 3rd-place teams |
| Calendar window | Week 39 to Week 43 |
| Regional qualifier period | Week 39 to Week 40 |
| Final tournament period | Week 41 to Week 43 |

## 8. VSC Regional Qualifiers

### 8.1 Regional Qualifier Summary

| Region | Participants | Paths | Qualifiers | Composition |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| APAC | 8 | 2 | 2 | China 3, Korea 2, Taiwan/Hong Kong 1, Japan 1, SEA 1 |
| EMEA | 16 | 4 | 4 | 8 leagues, 2 teams each |
| AMERICA | 8 | 2 | 2 | North America 4, LATAM 2, Brazil 2 |
| **Total** | **32** | **8** | **8** | - |

**Note:** AMERICA is treated as an 8-team qualifier because its listed slot breakdown and 2-path structure require 8 teams. If an upstream text says “2 teams participate,” that is treated as a typo.

### 8.2 Path Rules

Each regional qualifier is divided into 4-team Paths.

| Rule | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Path size | 4 teams |
| Format | Single elimination |
| Path semifinal | Bo3 |
| Path final | Bo5 |
| Qualifiers | One team per Path |
| Re-draw | No re-draw after Path assignment unless the draw is invalid |

### 8.3 Same-League Path Protection

The intended rule is that teams from the same domestic league should not be placed in the same Path. However, this is mathematically impossible in APAC and AMERICA because some leagues have more representatives than available Paths.

| Region | Issue | Applied policy |
|---|---|---|
| APAC | China has 3 teams but only 2 Paths | Spread Chinese teams as evenly as possible; one Path may contain 2 Chinese teams |
| AMERICA | North America has 4 teams but only 2 Paths | Put 2 North American teams in each Path; avoid same-league semifinal pairings |
| EMEA | 8 leagues have 2 teams each and 4 Paths exist | Strict same-league Path protection is possible and required |

Best-effort protection order:

1. Avoid same-league teams in the same Path where mathematically possible.
2. If impossible, minimize same-league clustering.
3. Avoid same-league semifinal pairings inside a Path.
4. Use random draw only after the above constraints are satisfied.

## 12. Final Decision

The selected Week 39 to Week 43 plan keeps WT as the main event and integrates VSC without disturbing the WT climax.

Recommended headline schedule:

| Week | WT Main Content | VSC Content |
|---:|---|---|
| W39 | WT Group Stage first half plus Group A Final Day | VSC qualifier semifinals |
| W40 | WT Group B to Group H Final Days | VSC qualifier finals |
| W41 | WT Round of 16 | VSC Round of 16 and QF1 |
| W42 | WT Quarterfinals | VSC QF2 to QF4 before WT quarterfinals start |
| W43 | WT Semifinals and Grand Final | VSC Semifinals and Grand Final |

WT Grand Final remains the final match of the championship window on **Week 43 Sunday**. VSC Grand Final is placed on **Week 43 Saturday**, giving the second-tier cup its own spotlight without colliding with the WT title match.
