# L_MEAF League Format PRD

**Document name:** L_MEAF League Format PRD  
**Version:** v1.0  
**League code:** L_MEAF  
**League type:** 8-team single-venue split tournament circuit  
**Primary purpose:** Define the annual operation format for L_MEAF, including five split tournaments, point allocation, MM qualification, and final playoffs.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

L_MEAF is an 8-team league played at a single venue. Unlike standard spring/summer league formats, L_MEAF runs five split tournaments across the year. Teams earn circuit points from each split tournament. Those points are used to determine:

1. **MM Qualifier participants** after Split 3.
2. **Final Playoff participants** after Split 5.

The format is designed as a compact tournament circuit. Each split tournament must finish within four weeks. The default operating model finishes each split in **three weeks**, using mostly **Friday, Saturday, and Sunday** matchdays.

## 4. Split Tournament Format

Each of the five split tournaments uses the same 8-team double elimination bracket.

### 4.1 Default Seeding

| Split | Seeding basis |
|---|---|
| Split 1 | Preseason seeding or administrative draw |
| Split 2-5 | Current cumulative circuit points before the split |

If teams are tied on cumulative points for split seeding, use the most recent split placement as the first tiebreaker. If still tied, use head-to-head if applicable, then administrative draw.

### 4.2 Recommended 8-Team Bracket

| Upper Bracket Match | Pairing |
|---|---|
| UB QF A | Seed 1 vs Seed 8 |
| UB QF B | Seed 4 vs Seed 5 |
| UB QF C | Seed 2 vs Seed 7 |
| UB QF D | Seed 3 vs Seed 6 |

The bracket proceeds as a standard double elimination tournament without a bracket reset.

## 5. Default Split Tournament Schedule Template

Each split tournament is scheduled over three weeks, primarily on Friday, Saturday, and Sunday. A fourth week may be used only as an emergency buffer if the global calendar allows it.

| Split Week | Day | Stage | Matches | Format |
|---|---|---|---:|---|
| Week A | Friday | Upper Quarterfinals A-B | 2 | Bo3 |
| Week A | Saturday | Upper Quarterfinals C-D | 2 | Bo3 |
| Week A | Sunday | Lower Round 1 | 2 | Bo3 |
| Week B | Friday | Upper Semifinals | 2 | Bo3 |
| Week B | Saturday | Lower Round 2 | 2 | Bo3 |
| Week B | Sunday | Upper Final + Lower Semifinal | 2 | Bo3 |
| Week C | Friday | Lower Final | 1 | Bo3 |
| Week C | Saturday | Media / buffer day | 0 | None |
| Week C | Sunday | Grand Final | 1 | Bo5 |

This template keeps every split within three weeks while preserving the Friday-Saturday-Sunday event rhythm.

## 7. MM Qualifier

### 7.1 Eligibility

The MM Qualifier uses results from Split 1, Split 2, and Split 3.

Three teams qualify:

1. The top teams by cumulative points after Split 3.
2. The Split 3 champion is guaranteed a spot even if its points are not high enough.
3. The field size remains exactly three teams.

If the Split 3 champion is not already in the top three by points, the champion is inserted into the field and the lowest-ranked point qualifier is displaced.

### 7.2 MM Qualifier Tiebreakers

If teams are tied on points for qualification or seeding, use:

1. Better Split 3 placement.
2. Higher number of split tournament wins across Splits 1-3.
3. Higher number of top-2 finishes across Splits 1-3.
4. Head-to-head if applicable.
5. Administrative tiebreaker match or draw.

### 7.3 MM Qualifier Seeding

After the three-team field is determined, teams are seeded by cumulative points. The Split 3 placement tiebreaker applies if needed.

| Seed | Basis |
|---:|---|
| Seed 1 | Highest point-ranked qualifier |
| Seed 2 | Second-highest point-ranked qualifier |
| Seed 3 | Third-highest point-ranked qualifier |

### 7.4 MM Qualifier Format

The MM Qualifier is a two-match gauntlet.

| Day | Match | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Saturday | Seed 2 vs Seed 3 | Bo5 |
| Sunday | Seed 1 vs Saturday winner | Bo5 |

The Sunday winner qualifies for MM.

## 8. Final Playoff

### 8.1 Eligibility

The Final Playoff uses cumulative points from Split 1 through Split 5.

Five teams qualify:

1. The top teams by cumulative points after Split 5.
2. The Split 5 champion is guaranteed entry as **Seed 1**, even if its point total is not top five.
3. The field size remains exactly five teams.

If the Split 5 champion is not already in the top five by points, the champion is inserted as Seed 1 and the lowest-ranked point qualifier is displaced.

### 8.2 Final Playoff Tiebreakers

If teams are tied on points for qualification or seeding, use:

1. Better Split 5 placement.
2. Higher number of split tournament wins across Splits 1-5.
3. Higher number of top-2 finishes across Splits 1-5.
4. Higher cumulative points earned in Splits 4-5.
5. Head-to-head if applicable.
6. Administrative tiebreaker match or draw.

### 8.3 Final Playoff Seeding

| Seed | Basis |
|---:|---|
| Seed 1 | Split 5 champion |
| Seed 2 | Highest remaining point-ranked qualifier |
| Seed 3 | Second-highest remaining point-ranked qualifier |
| Seed 4 | Third-highest remaining point-ranked qualifier |
| Seed 5 | Fourth-highest remaining point-ranked qualifier |

### 8.4 Final Playoff Format

The Final Playoff uses the same 5-team ladder structure as L_JP and L_TW.

All matches are Bo5.

| Round | Day | Match | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| Round 1 | Saturday | Seed 4 vs Seed 5 | Bo5 |
| Round 2 | Sunday | Seed 3 vs Round 1 winner | Bo5 |
| Round 3 | Saturday | Seed 2 vs Round 2 winner | Bo5 |
| Round 4 | Sunday | Seed 1 vs Round 3 winner | Bo5 |

The Round 4 winner is the L_MEAF season champion.

## 10. Operating Principles

1. L_MEAF is a tournament circuit, not a round-robin league.
2. Split tournaments are the primary source of points.
3. Split 3 champion protection exists for MM relevance.
4. Split 5 champion protection exists for final-season momentum and automatically grants Seed 1 in the Final Playoff.
5. The preferred event rhythm is Friday-Saturday-Sunday.
6. The official split tournament template is three weeks long, with a fourth week available only as an emergency buffer when the global calendar allows it.
7. Bracket reset is not used by default in split Grand Finals.
8. MM Qualifier and Final Playoff do not award additional split points unless the league office adds a separate championship-point layer.
